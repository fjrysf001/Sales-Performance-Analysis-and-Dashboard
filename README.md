# Sales-Performance-Analysis-and-Dashboard
📊 Sales Performance Analysis & Dashboard (2003–2005)

📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes historical sales data from 2003 to 2005 to identify business trends, evaluate product and regional performance, and generate actionable insights to support strategic decision-making. The project covers the full data analysis workflow, from data cleaning to visualization and business recommendations.

🎯 Objectives
Understand sales trends over time
Identify top-performing countries, products, and customers
Detect seasonal patterns and revenue drivers
Provide data-driven business recommendations
🛠 Tools & Technologies
Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning, Analysis, Visualization)
Pivot Tables
VLOOKUP / INDEX-MATCH
Data Validation & Conditional Formatting

📂 Dataset
Source: Kaggle
Total Records: 2,823 rows
Period: 2003–2005
Features include: Order Number, Quantity, Price, Product Line, Country, Sales, and more
🔧 Data Cleaning & Preparation
Removed duplicate records and handled missing values
Standardized data formats (dates, product categories, pricing)
Validated data consistency to ensure accuracy
Created calculated fields (e.g., total sales, revenue metrics)
Prepared structured dataset for analysis and reporting

🔍 Analysis Process
Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA)
Analyzed sales performance by year, country, and product category
Identified trends, seasonal patterns, and anomalies
Built dashboard to visualize key performance indicators (KPIs)
Generated insights and business recommendations

📈 Key Insights
USA contributed over 36% of total sales, making it the top-performing market
Classic Cars was the highest-selling product category, while Trains and Ships had the lowest contribution
Sales increased significantly in 2004, indicating strong growth
A sharp decline occurred in 2005, suggesting potential business issues
Q4 consistently generated the highest revenue, with November as the peak month

💡 Business Recommendations
Enhance Q4 campaigns with targeted promotions and bundled offers
Investigate the root cause of the 2005 sales decline (pricing, competition, supply issues)
Diversify sales strategies to improve performance in Q1–Q3
Implement data-driven forecasting for inventory and demand planning
Strengthen customer engagement to reduce dependency on seasonal sales
